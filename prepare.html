Prepare

Before you get started, think about why you would like to study in the US. Is it the best option for your future? What do you need to know about the university? What are coaches looking for? What do you need to know about the team? Set goals and priorities and stick to them.

Which university is right for you?

Before you speak to teams, think about the practical issues that will influence whether or not a particular university will be a choice for you? Below is a list of things you might take into consideration. Is there anything you would add to this list? What would be a deal-breaker for you?

Define your deal-makers and deal-breakers

  • Cost

    Will you need a grant or scholarship to be able to study in the US?

  • Team Culture

    Think about what you like about your own club, and what you want to know about the team culture. The coaches will be keen to put you in touch with team members, but if they don't, ask to be.

  • NCAA Ranking

    Is it important to be on a top Divison 1 team, or do you just want to row?

  • Academic Recognition and Standing

    Is this university well known in the UK? Will your qualifications be recognized How does the course content of US majors compare to those in the UK?

  • Academic Offer

    Look at the departments, faculty, research facilities, opportunities for undergraduate research, internships and career services.

  • Academic requirements

    Do you meet the academic requirements of the university? Beware that some universities don't accept a PE A level.

  • Size of the campus:

    Consider whether you want to be on a large or small campus, live in a rural or urban environment.

  • Geography

    Keep in mind that if you go to a northern school, it gets COLD and you'll do a lot of indoor training. Think about other practicalities such as proximity to international airports.

  • Campus Life

    Ask to be put in contact with a student from the UK to find out about things like the social life, dormitories and dining halls.

  • Support for international students

    Find out what support the university can offer you and how many international students study there.

  • Alumni networks and career services

    The strength and activity of alumni networks tells a lot about the university. Are there active alumni networks in the UK you can tap into?

Writing your rowing CV

There are all sorts of ways to do this. You can prepare a document to use as an attachment or create a free website. Whether or not you have a rowing CV, coaches will still ask you to fill out their own recruiting form, but it's nice to have something ready to send off at a moment's notice.

Here's what coaches want to know

  • Personal details

    Including name, date of birth and contact informaton.

  • Erg Scores

    Include your 2000m score as a minimum. Some universities ask for 6000m results. Don't worry about this, just include any pieces you are regularly tested on so you can show improvement.

  • Racing Results

    Include status events like National Schools Regatta, the Scullery, Schools Head of the River, The Metropolitan Regatta, Henley Women's or Henley Royal. Many US coaches will know these events well. Include any GB trialing experience.

  • Physcial attributes

    Include height, weight and arm span.

  • Coach's contact details

    Get permission first.

  • School name and address
  • Anticipated graduation date
  • Grade point average

    Most recruiting forms request this, you can go to this NCAA document and work it out, but do explain how you've done this.

  • Your GCSE and A level subjects

    Beware that some universities don't recognize the PE A level.

  • GCSE Results
  • SAT or ACT results

    These are standardized tests that most universities have required in the past, but were made optional or waived during Covid. There has been some talk of phasing them out altogether. Many universities give you a choice between one or the other. The SAT has the benefit of having a free SAT practice materials via Khan Academy (see Further Resources).

  • Academic Honours

    Ask to be put in contact with a student from the UK to find out about things like the social life, dormitories and dining halls.

  • Videos

    This is not strictly necessary, but if you would like to include some video material, you can do so easily by uploading it to YouTube and providing a link to coaches. You can do this using private settings. Make sure that it is good quality. Ask your coach to take some video during training.
