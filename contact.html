Make Contact

Once you've decided it go ahead, it's time to contact teams at the universities that interest you. Find out if you're a good fit for each other by speaking to coaches, team members and ideally, by making campus visits. Think about your goals, and consider what questions you should ask them? Think about how you will present yourself.

Questions to ask coaches

Remember that recruitment is a two-way process, and you are also interviewing them. You've thought about what you want from a university and team in the last section. What do you need to now find out to determine if it's a good fit? Avoid asking questions you can easily find answers to on their website.

  • How would you describe the team culture?
  • What does a typical training week look like?
  • What kind of athletes succeed in your rowing program?
  • How many days of classes need to be missed for races?
  • What do I need to achieve to be considered a top recruit?
  • Can I still participate in GB trials and U23 rowing?
  • What is the team grade point average?
  • Is there someone on the team with my major?
  • What is the team retention rate?
  • What is the team record with injuries?
  • Are medical costs included?
  • What financial support is available?

Sing your praises

This is not the right time to be understated. There's a lot of competition for about 10-15 places on a team. The coaches will only know what you choose to tell them, so it is not the time to be modest. Coaches need to build a case for the Admissions team as to why they should accept you. Let the coaches know about your rowing, academic and any other extra-curricular successes.

You’ll need teacher recommendations for your university application, you'll want to give your teachers similar advice. American teachers will write sentences like, "This is the best student of my career."

The bottom line is that coaches are looking for rowers who are excited about being part of their team. You need to communicate that excitement. Don't make them wonder if you’re interested or why you want or deserve to be on the team.
