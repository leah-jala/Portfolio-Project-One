Make Contact

Once you've decided it go ahead, it's time to contact teams at the universities that interest you. Find out if you're a good fit for each other by speaking to coaches, team members and ideally, by making campus visits. Think about your goals and consider what questions you should ask them? Your style of communication will be important.

Questions to ask coaches

Recruitment is a two-way process, and you are also interviewing them. What do you need to now find out to determine if the team is a good fit? Avoid asking questions you can easily find answers to on their website. Review the goals you made in stage one and prepare a list.

Here are some ideas to get you started

  • How would you describe the team culture?
  • What does a typical training week look like?
  • What kind of athletes succeed in your rowing program?
  • How many days of classes need to be missed for races?
  • What do I need to achieve to be considered a top recruit?
  • Can I still participate in GB trials and U23 rowing?
  • What is the team grade point average?
  • Is there someone on the team with my major?
  • What is the team retention rate?
  • What is the team record with injuries?
  • Are medical costs included?
  • What financial support is available?

A Note on Communication - Sing your Praises!

"You need to be much more animated verbally and in email. Compared to Brits, Americans are very gung-ho!"
Annalisa, Tideway Scullers School, 2021

This is not the right time to be modest. There's a lot of competition for about 10-15 places on a team. The coaches will only know what you choose to tell them, so it is not the time to be modest. Coaches also need to build a case for the Admissions team as to why they should accept you. Show pride in your rowing, academic and any other extra-curricular successes.
